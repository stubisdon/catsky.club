# Music Artist Psychology, V1

## What People Actually Desire

In order of when each desire kicks in:

1. **Feel something on demand.** Music’s first job is emotional regulation: heartbreak processed, hype summoned, nostalgia triggered, focus held. This entry-point desire is satisfied by the recording and has been commoditized to nearly free by streaming. Audio itself cannot be the revenue source because it serves the cheapest, most substitutable desire.
2. **Be understood.** When an artist articulates something a listener feels but cannot say, the relationship shifts from “I like this song” to “this person gets me.” Attachment starts here, and it attaches to a person with a story—not a sound. Authenticity is load-bearing: people attach to you, and they can smell a constructed vacancy.
3. **Define and signal identity.** Taste is identity technology. “I listen to X” means “I am the kind of person who…” Being early to an artist is social currency: “I knew them before.” Fans share music to say something about themselves, not to help the artist.
4. **Belong.** Fandom is a tribe. A show is collective effervescence: hundreds of strangers feeling the same thing in the same room. People are starving for this and will pay heavily for it.
5. **Be close to, and seen by, someone they admire.** Parasocial intimacy includes replies, recognition, access, and “they know my name.” The path from follower to superfan follows a gradient of desired closeness.
6. **Matter to the story.** In patronage psychology, superfans do not buy products; they fund a narrative in which they are invested: “I helped this happen.” The payment itself is meaningful.

## How the Business Model Maps to Those Desires

Each revenue stream makes one desire purchasable.

| Desire | What they buy |
| --- | --- |
| Feel something | Streams: near-free; marketing, not revenue. |
| Identity signal | Merch (a badge, not clothing); vinyl (a devotion artifact). |
| Belonging | Tickets, memberships, community. |
| Closeness / being seen | VIP, meet-and-greets, Patreon/Discord tiers, replies. |
| Status via scarcity | Limited editions, early access, front row. |
| Mattering to the story | Crowdfunding, patronage, “keep going” support. |

### Career Equation

The free layer—recordings, videos, and story—serves desires 1–2 at scale and builds attachment. The paid layer sells desires 3–6 to people who have become attached.

> Monetization is not charging for content; it is giving emotionally invested people ways to express that investment.

## The Deepest Contrast with SaaS Instincts

A SaaS customer pays to get a job done and wants to feel nothing: price is pure friction, minimized and forgotten. A fan pays to feel more, and the act of paying is part of the product: devotion made visible and participation in the story.

Superfans want to give. The job is to make giving meaningful, not to optimize a checkout. This is why a paid video unlock is psychologically backwards: it puts a toll booth on stages 1–2, the free intimacy-building layer, while offering nothing for desires 3–6, where willingness to pay lives.

## The Two Jobs

| Job | Focus |
| --- | --- |
| **Creative job** | Articulate a specific feeling for a specific kind of person, wrapped in a visible human story they can invest in. |
| **Business job** *(deferred)* | Once people are invested, sell belonging, closeness, identity artifacts, and a way to matter to the story. |

> Never sell the feelings themselves; those must stay free.
