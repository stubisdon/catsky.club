## Pillar 3: Negotiation Leverage

### Creating Optionality

Your leverage increases when you have options:

1. **Documentation leverage**: You know how everything works
2. **Relationship leverage**: Members know and trust your work
3. **Technical leverage**: Systems depend on your architecture decisions
4. **Market leverage**: Other opportunities exist for your skills
5. **Time leverage**: Replacement would take months

### Equity Conversation Framework

**When to have the conversation:**
- After a clear win (revenue milestone, member milestone)
- Before Phase 2 begins (leverage your Phase 1 contribution)
- When external opportunity creates urgency

**How to frame it:**
1. Start with shared success: "We've built something real together"
2. Name your contribution specifically: "I've built X, Y, Z systems..."
3. Connect to future value: "For Phase 2 to succeed, we need..."
4. Make the ask: "I want to formalize my stake as 25%"
5. Offer structure: vesting, cliff, performance triggers

**Preparation:**
- Know your BATNA (Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement)
- Know the company's valuation assumptions
- Know standard equity ranges for your role type
- Have a written proposal ready

## Risk Analysis

### Risks to Equity Claim

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Founder disagrees on equity value | Medium | Document market rates, your alternatives |
| Company doesn't formalize equity | Medium | Get agreement in writing, even informal |
| Product fails before equity matters | Medium | Ensure your time investment is reasonable |
| Another contributor claims similar stake | Low | Differentiate your unique contributions |
| Relationship deteriorates | Low | Maintain professionalism, document everything |

### Signals to Watch

**Green flags:**
- Founder talks about "us" and "we"
- Your input is sought on major decisions
- Discussion of future roles and equity happens organically

**Yellow flags:**
- Decisions made without your input
- No discussion of long-term structure
- Vague answers about equity

**Red flags:**
- You're treated as a contractor, not a partner
- Founder avoids equity conversation
- Your work is undervalued or unacknowledged
